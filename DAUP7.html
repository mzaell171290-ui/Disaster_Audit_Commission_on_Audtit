DRRM
Assessment of Disaster Risk Reduction Management at the Local Level

A limited scoping and analysis of existing Disaster Risk Reduction and Management (DRRM) practices at the Local Government Unit level, drawing on existing literature, agency reports, and field validation. The assessment looks at how provincial, city, and municipal DRRM offices plan for, budget, and account for calamity funds, and identifies recurring gaps between what is allocated on paper and what actually reaches affected communities.

Key findings cover fund utilization rates, the timeliness of disbursements after a calamity declaration, and common documentation issues auditors encounter when tracing relief goods and cash aid back to their source appropriations.

TYPHOON YOLANDA
A Special Report on Typhoon Yolanda

This special report examines the disbursement and use of national and international relief funds following Typhoon Yolanda (Haiyan), one of the strongest storms on record to make landfall. It traces the flow of assistance from national agencies through local government units and down to the barangay level.

The report highlights lessons learned around emergency procurement rules, donor-fund reconciliation, and the rebuilding of damaged public infrastructure, with recommendations aimed at tightening controls for future large-scale disaster response.

CATEGORY
Citizen Participatory Audit

The Citizen Participatory Audit (CPA) program invites ordinary citizens, civil society groups, and community volunteers to take part directly in the audit process for disaster-related projects, from small flood-control structures to relief-goods warehousing.

By pairing trained COA auditors with community observers, the program builds local capacity to spot red flags early, such as ghost deliveries or overpriced materials, and strengthens public trust in how calamity funds are eventually accounted for.

FLOOD CONTROL
Audit of Flood Control Infrastructure Projects

A review of flood-control structures funded under national disaster mitigation programs, covering drainage systems, floodwalls, and pumping stations across flood-prone provinces. The audit checks whether completed structures match approved engineering plans and released funds.

Findings point to delayed completion timelines in several provinces and recommend stronger coordination between the Department of Public Works and Highways and local disaster councils.

RELIEF GOODS
Tracking Relief Goods Distribution

This report follows the movement of relief goods, food packs, and hygiene kits from national warehouses down to barangay distribution points after major typhoons. It cross-checks delivery receipts against beneficiary lists to spot gaps or duplication.

The audit recommends a standardized digital tracking system to replace manual logbooks currently used by most local government units.

EARLY WARNING
Evaluation of Early Warning Systems

An assessment of government-funded early warning equipment, including rain gauges, water level sensors, and community alert sirens installed in high-risk municipalities. The report examines maintenance records and response times during recent weather events.

It highlights several units left unmaintained since installation and recommends a regular inspection schedule tied to local disaster risk reduction budgets.

How can we help you?

What is the "Disaster Audit" site by the Commission on Audit, and what should I know about its purpose? ▾

The Disaster Audit site is an official Commission on Audit (COA) portal that publishes audit findings, summaries, and related documentation on disaster fund utilization.


Who should I contact for media, research, or official inquiries? ▾

Reach the COA Public Information Office through the contact details listed in the footer below.


How is personal or sensitive information handled on the site? ▾

All data shared through the portal is handled in accordance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012.
